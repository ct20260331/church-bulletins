Psalm 89:19-29

John Gill's Exposition of the Old Testament — Ordered Covenantal Exposition
Verse 19

Then thou spakest in vision to thy holy one, and saidst, I have laid help upon one that is mighty; I have exalted one chosen out of the people. Then thou spakest in vision to thy holy one,.... Samuel, or Nathan, to whom the Lord spoke in vision, or by a spirit of prophecy, concerning David, his choice, and unction, as a type of Christ, 1 Samuel 16:1 or rather the Lord's holy One, Jesus Christ Himself, who was with Him from all eternity, in whom the vision of all the prophets was fulfilled, and who is holy in both His natures, divine and human; to Him the Lord spoke, and with Him He counselled, about the salvation of His chosen people: and saidst, I have laid help upon one that is mighty; this "mighty" One is Christ, the mighty God, the mighty Man, the able Saviour, who is mighty to save to the uttermost; upon whom the Father "laid help" for His people; not that He needed help Himself, but they did; and He was able to give it, and did give it, by working out their salvation: I have exalted one chosen out of the people; chosen by the Father to be the Mediator and Head of the church, and exalted to be a Prince and a Saviour, in our nature, above all creatures.

  1. God originally spoke by revelation to His prophetic messengers or to His eternal, holy Son regarding the covenant of grace.
  2. Divine saving help was structurally placed upon Christ, who possesses absolute power and capability to deliver His helpless people.
  3. The Mediator was chosen from among the people and subsequently exalted as Head and Saviour over all creation.
Verse 20

I have found David my servant; with my holy oil have I anointed him. I have found David my servant,.... Not David literally, though he was a type of Christ, and was found among the sheepfolds, and anointed to be king; but Christ, the antitypical David, whom the Father "found" in His infinite wisdom and love, and appointed to be the Redeemer; who is His "servant" in the work of redemption, fulfilling His law and bringing glory to His name: with my holy oil have I anointed him; with the Holy Ghost, not by measure, but above his fellows; whereby He was consecrated to His offices of Prophet, Priest, and King, and qualified to discharge them in our nature.

  1. The historical David serves as an intentional type pointing directly to Jesus Christ, the true antitypical David.
  2. God the Father, in His eternal wisdom and love, found and appointed Christ to perform the work of redemption.
  3. Christ acts as a servant who perfectly fulfills the divine law and brings ultimate glory to the Father's name.
  4. The Holy Spirit was poured out immeasurably upon Christ to consecrate and qualify Him for His mediatorial offices.
Verse 21

With whom my hand shall be established: mine arm also shall strengthen him. With whom my hand shall be established,.... The hand of God's power and providential care should be constantly with David, and with Christ his antitype, to protect, support, and prosper Him in His work; which hand should be so firmly "established" with Him, that it should never be removed from Him: mine arm also shall strengthen him; with almighty power to carry Him through His arduous work of redemption, to bear up under His sufferings, and to conquer all His and His people's enemies.

  1. God's sovereign power and providential care remained firmly fixed upon Christ to sustain Him.
  2. The divine hand was established permanently and unalterably, ensuring it would never be removed from the Mediator.
  3. Almighty strength was supplied to bear Christ through the arduous work of redemption and intense sufferings.
  4. This omnipotent arm guaranteed final victory over all the spiritual enemies of Christ and His church.
Verse 22

The enemy shall not exact upon him; nor the son of wickedness afflict him. The enemy shall not exact upon him,.... Satan, the great enemy of Christ and His church, could not demand the debt of obedience or suffering from Him, for He owed none; nor could he deceive or overcome Him, though he attempted it: nor the son of wickedness afflict him; Judas, the son of perdition, or the wicked Jews, or the man of sin, the antichristian power; none of these could bring any affliction upon Him but what was by divine permission, and all their malicious designs were ultimately turned to His glory and the good of His people.

  1. Satan could not demand debt, obedience, or suffering from Christ, since the Savior owed him nothing.
  2. The great adversary utterly failed to deceive, master, or overcome Christ despite his relentless temptations.
  3. Wicked human agents, including Judas and antichristian powers, could not afflict Him without sovereign divine permission.
  4. All malicious designs aimed against the Mediator were providentially turned to His glory and His people's good.
Verse 23

And I will beat down his foes before his face, and plague them that hate him. And I will beat down his foes before his face,.... The Jews that rejected Him, the Roman empire that persecuted His church, and all antichristian powers; these the Lord promises to break in pieces and destroy before the face of Christ, by the power of His gospel and the judgments of His hand: and plague them that hate him; with temporal calamities and spiritual judgments here, and with everlasting destruction from His presence hereafter.

  1. God promised to break and utterly destroy all Christ's historic, imperial, and antichristian adversaries.
  2. This destruction of foes is accomplished through the power of the gospel and historic divine judgments.
  3. Those exhibiting hatred toward Christ are plagued with severe temporal calamities and spiritual miseries here.
  4. The final, ultimate punishment of these haters is eternal destruction away from the presence of the Lord.
Verse 24

But my faithfulness and my mercy shall be with him: and in my name shall his horn be exalted. But my faithfulness and my mercy shall be with him,.... "Faithfulness" in fulfilling all covenant promises made to Him as the Mediator; and "mercy" towards His mystical body, His church, which is dispensed through Him: these should never depart from Him, but remain as His constant attendants: and in my name shall his horn be exalted; His power, glory, and kingly authority should be advanced and made manifest by the power and authority of God Himself, to the terror of His enemies and the joy of His saints.

  1. Divine faithfulness ensures the strict fulfillment of all covenant promises made to Christ as Mediator.
  2. Covenant mercy is continually dispensed through Christ directly to His mystical body, which is the church.
  3. These two divine attributes remain the constant, inseparable companions of the Savior throughout His work.
  4. Christ's power, glory, and kingly authority are visibly advanced by the supreme authority of God's name.
Verse 25

I will set his hand also in the sea, and his right hand in the rivers. I will set his hand also in the sea, and his right hand in the rivers,.... Meaning that His dominion should be extended far and wide; from the Mediterranean Sea to the Euphrates and other rivers, in the case of David literally; and as for Christ, His kingdom should spread from sea to sea, and from the river to the ends of the earth, through the preaching of the gospel and the expansion of His spiritual empire over Jews and Gentiles alike.

  1. The geographical boundaries of David's historic dominion prefigured a far greater spiritual reality.
  2. Christ's spiritual kingdom extends universally from sea to sea and to the ends of the earth.
  3. This worldwide expansion is actively achieved through the powerful preaching of the Christian gospel.
  4. The resulting kingdom breaks national barriers, encompassing both Jews and Gentiles within one empire.
Verse 26

He shall cry unto me, Thou art my father, my God, and the rock of my salvation. He shall cry unto me, Thou art my Father,.... Christ, as man, could call God His Father, not only by creation and adoption, but by virtue of His unique filiation and covenant relation; which He did frequently in His prayers on earth: my God; who chose Him, covenant-bounded Him, and whom He loved, obeyed, and worshipped: and the rock of my salvation; the support, defense, and security of His human nature and His church in the work of redemption.

  1. Christ, in His human nature, addressed God as Father through an intimate, unique covenant relation.
  2. He consistently manifested this filial devotion during His earthly life through frequent, earnest prayers.
  3. God is identified as Christ's God, who chose Him and whom He perfectly loved and worshipped.
  4. The Father served as the dependable rock, supporting and securing Christ's humanity during redemption.
Verse 27

Also I will make him my firstborn, higher than the kings of the earth. Also I will make him my firstborn,.... Christ is the firstborn of every creature, the firstbegotten from the dead, and the chief of the family of God, having the preeminence in all things; He is the firstborn in regard to dignity, power, and inheritance: higher than the kings of the earth; possessing a kingdom more excellent, extensive, and enduring than any earthly monarch, being King of kings and Lord of lords.

  1. Christ holds absolute preeminence as the firstborn of creation and the firstbegotten from the dead.
  2. His unique status grants Him supreme dignity, power, and the primary inheritance over God's house.
  3. The Messiah is exalted far above all earthly monarchs, possessing an infinitely more excellent kingdom.
  4. His enduring sovereign dominion establishes Him definitively as King of kings and Lord of lords.
Verse 28

My mercy will I keep for him for evermore, and my covenant shall stand fast with him. My mercy will I keep for him for evermore,.... The mercy of God, which is the source of all covenant blessings, is safely deposited in Christ, the Head of the covenant, to be by Him communicated to His seed through all generations: and my covenant shall stand fast with him; it is an everlasting covenant, ordered in all things and sure; it stands firm on His account, because He has fulfilled its conditions and guarantees its blessings to His people.

  1. Divine mercy, the deep source of all covenant blessings, is safely deposited in Christ the Head.
  2. This preserved mercy is continuously communicated through Him to His spiritual seed across all generations.
  3. The covenant remains an everlasting arrangement that is completely ordered in all things and sure.
  4. It stands permanently firm because Christ perfectly fulfilled its conditions and guarantees its blessings.
Verse 29

His seed also will I make to endure for ever, and his throne as the days of heaven. His seed also will I make to endure for ever,.... His spiritual seed, the church of the firstborn, who are regenerated by His Word and Spirit; these shall never perish or be lost, but shall continue through all ages of time, and to all eternity: and his throne as the days of heaven; His spiritual kingdom and kingly authority over His church shall never cease, but shall endure as long as the heavens remain, and be consummated in the heavenly glory.

  1. Christ's spiritual seed consists of His true church, regenerated effectively by His Word and Spirit.
  2. This elect body of believers shall never perish, continuing safely through time and eternity.
  3. His spiritual throne and kingly authority over the church will never cease or diminish.
  4. This messianic kingdom endures permanently, finding its final consummation in everlasting heavenly glory.
Source Text: John Gill, An Exposition of the Old and New Testament (Public Domain, 1746–1763).
